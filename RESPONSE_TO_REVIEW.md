# Response to Reviewer

We thank the reviewer for an exceptionally careful and fair review. The central
critique is correct and we have restructured the paper around it: the synthetic
benchmark's data-generating process shares the estimator's functional form, so
it verifies *correctness and sensitivity*, not the real-world validity of the
shared-elasticity assumption -- and the previous abstract over-claimed. Every
point is addressed below; all numbers are regenerated by the released scripts.

## Major comments

**1. Near-circular validation (DGP = estimator's model).** Agreed and now stated
explicitly. The Experimental Design opens with a paragraph "What the synthetic
benchmark can and cannot show"; the Limitations lead with this as *the* primary
limitation; the abstract and conclusion now say the experiments "verify
correctness and quantify sensitivity rather than establishing the assumption in
the wild." We added a dedicated misspecification study (new section "Robustness
to Model Misspecification") as the complementary evidence.

**2. Wrong baseline / headline.** Agreed. We no longer lead with "17.6 % vs
uniform" as the method's contribution. The text now states plainly that proxy
weighting captures essentially all of the gain over uniform, while estimating
beta is statistically indistinguishable from raw-proxy (beta=1) on this
population and is therefore presented as a **robustness safeguard**, justified by
the beta-sweep, not as the source of the headline number.

**3. Weekly targets never validated.** Added. The leave-units-out protocol now
runs for **monthly and weekly** reporting periods (Table 2). Weekly within-period
R2 is 0.31 (vs 0.29 monthly); both are reported throughout.

**4. Daily R2 inflated by conserved totals.** Agreed. We now lead with a
**within-period (period-demeaned) R2** that isolates shape recovery and is 0 for
uniform by construction. The honest contribution is within-R2 ~ 0.29 (monthly) /
0.31 (weekly); the pooled daily R2 is explicitly described as inflated and
demoted. New Figure 4 plots within-period R2 by method and period.

**5. Fold variability understated.** Fixed. Cross-validation is now **repeated
over 30 random partitions** (x5 folds); we report mean +/- s.d. and the
2.5-97.5 % range across all 150 fold evaluations.

## Minor comments

- **Scale-dominated MAE.** Added scale-free metrics: per-brand relative MAE and
  MASE vs the uniform naive (~0.83 monthly, ~0.82 weekly). Raw MAE is no longer
  the headline.
- **sMAPE on low counts.** Reported alongside scale-free metrics.
- **No confounding in the DGP.** Now the core of the new misspecification study:
  a proxy-independent sales fraction phi and a non-log-linear link. As phi->1 the
  method degrades gracefully to the uniform baseline and never below it; under
  the saturating link beta-hat is attenuated and shape recovery drops (Figure 5).
- **Reference verification method.** Now stated in the paper (Data and Code
  Availability): references verified against publisher DOIs and >=2 indexers.
- **Conservation framing.** Reframed throughout as a *constraint* satisfied by
  all methods (including uniform), reported only as a correctness check.
- **Calibration vs. target representativeness.** Added a check and caveat: the
  splits are broadly comparable on proxy magnitude (mean 1.35k vs 1.47k) but
  calibration brands skew smaller (medians 0.75k vs 1.22k); stratified pooling is
  the mitigation.

## Questions for the authors

   (proxy-independent fraction + non-log-linear link).
2. **Within-month (shape-only) R2 and error?** Now the headline metric (Table 2,
